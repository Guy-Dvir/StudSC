## Information Architecture
*   **Home (The Entry):** High-impact hero video, value proposition, and curated portfolio highlights.
*   **Work (The Proof):** Filterable gallery of past projects.
    *   **Case Study Template:** Deep dive into problem, process, and measurable results.
*   **Services (The Solution):** Overview of core capabilities.
    *   **Brand Strategy:** Market positioning and voice.
    *   **Visual Identity:** Logo systems and brand guidelines.
    *   **Digital Experience:** Web design and creative technology.
*   **About (The Soul):** Narrative-driven company history, team bios, and core values.
*   **Journal (The Authority):** Thought leadership, industry trends, and studio updates.
*   **Contact (The Bridge):** Lead capture form and location details.

## Navigation Structure
*   **Primary Header:** Logo (Home), Work, Services, About, **Let’s Talk (CTA)**.
*   **Footer:** Newsletter opt-in, Social links (IG, LI), Privacy Policy, and Terms of Service.

## Primary User Flows
*   **The Prospect Path:** Home → Work → Specific Case Study → Services (Validation) → Contact (Conversion).
*   **The Talent Path:** Home → About (Culture Check) → Journal (Expertise) → Contact (Inquiry).
*   **The Direct Path:** Social Media → Journal Post → Related Case Study → Contact.

## Strategic Page Notes
*   **Dynamic Case Studies:** Every "Work" page must include a "Next Project" footer to keep users in the discovery loop.
*   **Conversion Points:** Strategic CTAs placed at the 50% scroll depth of the Services and About pages.
*   **Frictionless Contact:** The inquiry form should use conditional logic to qualify leads by budget and project type.
