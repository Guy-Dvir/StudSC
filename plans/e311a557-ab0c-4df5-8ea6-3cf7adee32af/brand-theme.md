## Color Palette
*   **Midnight Obsidian (#0F172A):** A deep, near-black blue used for primary backgrounds to create a sense of depth and premium exclusivity.
*   **Electric Sky (#0EA5E9):** A high-vibrancy cyan used sparingly for critical Call-to-Action (CTA) buttons and active states.
*   **Frosted Silver (#E2E8F0):** A cool-toned neutral for secondary text and borders, maintaining a clean, industrial feel.
*   **Glacier White (#F8FAFC):** The primary color for typography and high-contrast negative space to ensure maximum legibility.
*   **Cyber Mint (#2DD4BF):** A soft, futuristic accent color used for success states and subtle UI highlights.

## Typography Direction
*   **Headlines:** **Syne** (Extra Bold). A geometric sans-serif with unique character widths that communicates an avant-garde, trend-setting aesthetic.
*   **Body Copy:** **Inter**. A highly legible, modern typeface that balances the eccentricities of the headlines with a professional, "tech-first" utility.
*   **Labels/Technical Data:** **JetBrains Mono**. Used for price points and product specs to lean into a curated, "street-tech" vibe.

## Brand Personality
"COOL" is an effortless trendsetter. It positions itself as the quietest person in the room with the loudest influence. The brand is sophisticated, hyper-modern, and unapologetically minimalist, favoring curated quality over mass-market quantity.

## Tone of Voice
The copy is punchy, rhythmic, and confident. It avoids marketing fluff and industry jargon, opting instead for short, declarative statements. It speaks to the user as a peer, utilizing a "less is more" philosophy that assumes the audience already understands the value proposition.

## Visual Style References
*   **Brutalist Minimalism:** Large typography and stark layouts inspired by high-fashion lookbooks (e.g., Acne Studios).
*   **Industrial Precision:** Clean lines and "frosted glass" glassmorphism effects reminiscent of premium hardware interfaces.
*   **High-End Photography:** Desaturated imagery with high contrast, focusing on texture and silhouette rather than busy backgrounds.
